About

Taiwanese pineapple cake, or "fènglí sū" (鳳梨酥), is a popular traditional pastry that has become a symbol of Taiwanese hospitality and gifting culture. These small, square cakes feature a buttery, crumbly shortbread-like crust filled with a rich, tangy-sweet pineapple jam made from either pure pineapple or a blend with winter melon. Originally developed as a way to preserve fruit, pineapple cakes have grown into a beloved treat enjoyed during festivals and given as souvenirs. The pineapple, or "fènglí," is also associated with good fortune in Taiwanese culture, making these cakes not only delicious but also a meaningful gesture of luck and prosperity.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 200g all-purpose flour
  • 100g unsalted butter, softened
  • 50g powdered sugar
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 200g pineapple jam
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  2. In a bowl, mix the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In another bowl, cream the butter and powdered sugar until light and fluffy.
  4. Add the egg yolk and vanilla extract to the butter mixture and mix well.
  5. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients and mix until a dough forms.
  6. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
  7. Roll out the dough and cut into squares. Place a spoonful of pineapple jam in the center of each square.
  8. Fold the corners of the dough over the jam to form a square shape.
  9. Brush with egg wash and bake for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.
  10. Let cool before serving. Enjoy!
