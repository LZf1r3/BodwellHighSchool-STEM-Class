About

Taiwanese noodles are a cornerstone of the island’s rich and diverse culinary heritage, reflecting influences from Chinese regional cuisines while incorporating unique local ingredients and flavors. From comforting beef noodle soup—a national favorite featuring tender braised beef, hearty broth, and chewy noodles—to the simple yet savory danzi noodles, traditionally served with minced pork and shrimp, Taiwanese noodle dishes showcase a perfect balance of texture and taste. Whether stir-fried, served in soup, or enjoyed cold, these noodles are a beloved staple found everywhere from bustling night markets to home kitchens, symbolizing the island’s deep connection to comfort food and street cuisine.

Recipe

Ingredients

  • 500g beef shank, cut into chunks
  • 200g noodles (wheat or rice)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 piece ginger, sliced
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions for garnish
  • Pickled mustard greens (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a pot, heat some oil and sauté the onion, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Add the beef chunks and brown them on all sides.
  3. Add the star anise, cinnamon stick, soy sauce, and beef broth. Bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef is tender.
  5. Cook the noodles according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. Once the beef is tender, season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the soup over the cooked noodles, garnished with chopped green onions and pickled mustard greens if desired.
  8. Enjoy your delicious Taiwanese beef noodle soup!
